# Sports Equipment Policy

**Document ID:** POL-SPT-001
**Last updated:** 2026-01-18

## General rules

Sports equipment may be carried as checked baggage on SkyLine Airways flights, subject to advance booking and the standard size and weight rules. Items not booked in advance may be refused at check-in if hold space is unavailable.

Booking must be made at least 48 hours before departure via Manage My Booking or customer services.

## Equipment-specific rules

### Bicycles

- One bicycle per passenger, max 32kg
- Must be packed in a hard or soft bicycle case
- Pedals removed, handlebars turned, tyres deflated
- Fee: GBP 60 (short-haul) / GBP 90 (long-haul)

### Skis and snowboards

- One set per passenger (skis + boots + poles, OR snowboard + boots)
- Max combined weight 23kg
- Must be packed in a dedicated ski or snowboard bag
- Fee: GBP 50 (short-haul) / GBP 75 (long-haul)

### Golf equipment

- One golf bag per passenger, max 23kg
- Must be packed in a hard or padded golf travel case
- Fee: GBP 45 (short-haul) / GBP 70 (long-haul)

### Surfboards and paddleboards

- Up to 2 boards per passenger in a single bag, max 32kg total
- Maximum length 277cm
- Fee: GBP 70 (short-haul) / GBP 100 (long-haul)
- Boards over 277cm cannot be accepted

### Diving equipment

- Treated as standard checked baggage
- Compressed air cylinders must be empty and valves removed
- Knives and spearguns must be packed securely

### Firearms (sporting use only)

- Must be declared and pre-approved at least 7 days before travel
- Firearm must be unloaded, in a locked hard case
- Ammunition (max 5kg) must be in original packaging in a separate locked case
- Fee: GBP 80 per direction
- Not accepted to all destinations; check restrictions before booking

## Items that count towards baggage allowance

Sports equipment fees are in addition to standard checked baggage fees. The sports equipment item itself does not count towards your standard checked baggage allowance, but additional regular baggage is charged at standard excess baggage rates.
