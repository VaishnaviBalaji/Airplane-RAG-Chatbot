# Special Assistance

**Document ID:** POL-ASS-001
**Last updated:** 2026-01-30

## Requesting assistance

Passengers requiring special assistance should notify SkyLine Airways at least 48 hours before departure. Requests can be made during booking, via the Manage My Booking section of the website, or by calling customer services.

## Mobility assistance

Wheelchair assistance is provided free of charge from the airport entrance to the aircraft seat and on arrival. Passengers may bring their own wheelchair, which will be stowed in the hold at no charge and returned at the destination gate where possible.

Battery-powered wheelchairs are accepted, but the battery type must be declared in advance. Lithium batteries above 300Wh are not permitted.

## Visual and hearing impairments

Passengers with visual or hearing impairments may travel with a recognised assistance dog at no charge, provided the appropriate documentation is presented at check-in. Assistance dogs travel in the cabin at the passenger's feet.

## Unaccompanied minors

Children aged 5 to 11 travelling alone must use the Unaccompanied Minor service, charged at GBP 50 per flight. The service includes:

- Escort through the airport
- Onboard supervision by cabin crew
- Handover to a designated adult at the destination

Children aged 12 to 15 may travel as adults but the Unaccompanied Minor service is available on request. Children under 5 cannot travel alone.

## Medical conditions

Passengers with medical conditions that may affect travel must complete a Medical Information Form (MEDIF) at least 72 hours before departure. This applies to recent surgery, oxygen requirements, and certain cardiac and respiratory conditions.

## Dietary requirements

Special meals (vegetarian, vegan, kosher, halal, gluten-free, diabetic) are available on long-haul flights and must be requested at least 24 hours before departure. No special meals are provided on short-haul flights, but passengers may bring their own food.
